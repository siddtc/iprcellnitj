Intellectual Property Rights.

Intellectual Property Rights (IPRs) are legal monopolistic rights conferred by the Government to inventors or creators of intellectual Property in industrial, scientific, literary & artistic fields in lieu of complete disclosure of the same. IPRs are territorial rights and there is no global patent.

Types Of PRs

  1. Patent : Protects Inventions eg. mobile phone technology, mechanical devices.
  2. Copyright: Protects expression of Ideas in tangible form eg. books, music, software.
  3. Trademark: Protects indication of source of goods or services eg. Pepsi, IBM.
  4. Design: Protects outer appearance of a trade article eg. car designs, phone designs.
  5. Geographical Indication: Protects geographic source of goods eg Phulkari, Kullu Shawl.
  6. New Plant Variety: Protects new plant variety eg new wheat variety.
  7. Semiconductor layout Design: Protects layout design of semiconductors.
  8. Trade Secrets: Protects know-how, non-patentable inventions eg. Coca-Cola Recipe.

PIC, PSCST

Patent Information Centre (PIC) at PSCST is a joint project of GoI & GoP to address to IPR needs of Punjab & Chandigarh. The facilities provided by PIC, PSCST are as follows:

  1. International Patent Search on nominal charges from 80+ countries
  2. Free Financial, Legal & Technical support for IPR (especially patents) filing, prosecution & renewal to all academicians .
  3. Technology Scan Studies & Patent information for technology up-gradation/ adoption.
  4. IPR Training.
  5. Consultancy services on various IPR filing.
  6. Awareness through Workshops/Lectures/ Seminars.
